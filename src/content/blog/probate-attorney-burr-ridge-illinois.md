# Probate Attorney in Burr Ridge, IL: Handling High-Value Estates in DuPage and Cook Counties

Burr Ridge sits on the border of DuPage and Cook Counties, and it's one of the wealthier communities in the Chicago suburbs. Estates here often involve significant real estate, investment portfolios, business interests, and complex asset structures. If you're dealing with a probate matter in Burr Ridge, here's what you should know.

## Which Court Handles Burr Ridge Probate?

Because Burr Ridge straddles two counties, which court handles probate depends on where the deceased lived:

- **DuPage County portion of Burr Ridge** — probate filed at DuPage County Circuit Court in Wheaton
- **Cook County portion of Burr Ridge** — probate filed at Cook County Circuit Court Probate Division in Chicago

If you're not sure which applies, check the deceased's driver's license or voter registration — the county listed there will determine the correct court. An attorney can also confirm this quickly.

## What Makes Burr Ridge Estates Different

Burr Ridge estates tend to be more complex than average for a few reasons:

**Higher property values.** Residential real estate in Burr Ridge regularly exceeds $1 million. At this level, appraisals, negotiations, and title work require more care, and the stakes for the executor are higher.

**Illinois estate tax threshold.** Illinois taxes estates over $4 million — one of the lowest thresholds in the country. Burr Ridge estates, particularly those combining real estate, retirement accounts, and investment portfolios, frequently approach or exceed this level. Proper estate planning and administration can significantly reduce this exposure.

**Business interests.** Many Burr Ridge residents own businesses. Valuing and transferring business interests through probate adds a layer of complexity that requires experienced legal counsel.

**Multi-asset estates.** Coordinating the sale of real estate, distribution of investment accounts, and transfer of personal property across multiple beneficiaries takes careful administration.

## The Probate Process for Burr Ridge Families

Whether your case is in DuPage or Cook County, the Illinois probate process follows the same general framework:

- Open the estate with the circuit court and receive Letters of Office
- Take inventory of all estate assets
- Publish notice to creditors (6-month claim period)
- Pay valid debts and taxes
- Distribute remaining assets to heirs per the will or Illinois intestacy law

For complex Burr Ridge estates, independent administration — which gives the executor broad authority without court approval at each step — is particularly valuable. It keeps the process moving and reduces court costs.

Not every probate attorney is equipped for high-value or complex estates. For a Burr Ridge matter, look for an attorney with:

- Familiarity with Illinois estate tax and strategies to minimize it
- Experience with real estate sales at higher price points
- Clear communication and a defined process for keeping complex estates on track
