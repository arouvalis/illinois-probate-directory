## Understanding Probate in Northern Illinois

If your loved one passed away in Lee County, Ogle County, or another northern Illinois county, the probate process will be handled through that county's circuit court. Each county has slightly different procedures and timelines, which is why working with an attorney familiar with local courts can be beneficial.

In Illinois, probate is generally required when a deceased person owned assets solely in their name worth more than $100,000, or when they owned real estate without a co-owner or transfer-on-death designation. The process involves validating the will (if one exists), identifying and valuing assets, paying debts and taxes, and distributing remaining assets to beneficiaries.

A knowledgeable probate attorney can guide you through each step, helping you avoid costly mistakes and delays.
