# McHenry County Probate and Estate Lawyers: Finding Help When You Need It

When a family member dies in McHenry County, the legal questions come fast. Do you need to go to court? Who's in charge of the estate? What happens to the house? Most families haven't been through this before, and knowing where to start makes a real difference.

This guide explains what kinds of attorneys handle estate matters in McHenry County, what to look for when choosing one, and where to find qualified local help.

## What Type of Lawyer Do You Need?

After a death, families in McHenry County typically need one of two types of attorneys — and often the same person handles both:

**Probate attorney** — handles the court process of administering a deceased person's estate. This includes filing the petition to open the estate, managing creditor notifications, handling real property transfers, and guiding the executor through to final distribution. If the deceased owned a home or had significant assets, this is who you need.

**Estate planning attorney** — helps living clients create wills, trusts, and powers of attorney. If you're dealing with a death right now, you need a probate attorney. If you're planning ahead for your own family, you need estate planning. Many attorneys in McHenry County handle both.

You generally don't need a separate real estate attorney for probate — your probate attorney handles deed transfers and title matters as part of the estate administration.

## What Makes a Good Probate Attorney in McHenry County

Not all attorneys are equally well-suited for probate work. A few things to look for:

**Illinois licensure and probate focus** — probate law has specific rules, deadlines, and court procedures. An attorney who handles probate regularly will move your case forward more efficiently than a generalist.

**McHenry County Circuit Court familiarity** — probate cases in McHenry County are handled in Woodstock. An attorney who appears there regularly knows the local procedures and requirements.

**Clear communication** — probate takes months, sometimes longer. You want an attorney who explains what's happening at each stage and responds when you have questions. This matters more than most people realize when you're in the middle of a stressful process.

**Transparent fees** — ask about fee structure upfront. In Illinois, probate attorney fees are typically paid from the estate rather than out of pocket, but you should understand what you're agreeing to before signing anything.
