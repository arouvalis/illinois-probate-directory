## Palos Heights and Cook County Probate

Palos Heights is located in Cook County, which means all probate cases for Palos Heights residents are filed at the **Cook County Circuit Court Probate Division**, located at the Richard J. Daley Center, 50 W Washington St, Chicago, IL 60602.

Cook County handles more probate cases than any other county in Illinois. Its court has specific procedures and filing requirements that an experienced local attorney will know well. Even though the courthouse is in Chicago, many attorneys serving Palos Heights and the southwest suburbs practice regularly in Cook County Probate Court.

## The Illinois Probate Process for Palos Heights Residents

Here is what the probate process typically looks like for a Palos Heights estate:

**Weeks 1-6:** The executor files a petition at the Daley Center. The court validates the will, appoints the executor, and issues Letters of Office — the legal document authorizing the executor to manage and sell estate assets.

**Months 1-6:** The executor publishes a notice to creditors. Illinois law gives creditors 6 months from publication to file claims against the estate.

**Months 6-14:** Once the creditor period closes, the executor pays valid debts, prepares a final accounting, and petitions the court for distribution to heirs.

The mandatory 6-month creditor period is the primary driver of the timeline. A straightforward Palos Heights estate typically takes 12 to 18 months from filing to closing.

## When Do You Need a Probate Attorney in Palos Heights?

Not every estate requires formal probate, but most do when real property is involved. Common situations that require legal help include:

**The estate includes a home.** If the deceased owned property solely in their name, it must go through probate before it can be sold or transferred to heirs. This is the most common reason Palos Heights families seek probate counsel.

**There is no will.** When someone dies intestate (without a will), Illinois law determines how assets are distributed. An attorney helps navigate the process and ensures the right heirs are identified.

**Family members disagree.** Disputes among beneficiaries or questions about the validity of a will require legal representation to resolve properly.
