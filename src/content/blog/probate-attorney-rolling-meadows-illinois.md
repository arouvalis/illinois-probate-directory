## Rolling Meadows and Cook County Probate

Rolling Meadows is a Cook County suburb in the northwest Chicago area. Because it falls within Cook County, all probate cases for Rolling Meadows residents are filed at the **Cook County Circuit Court Probate Division**, located at the Richard J. Daley Center in Chicago.

Cook County handles more probate cases than any other county in Illinois. Its court has specific procedures, filing requirements, and timelines that an experienced local attorney will know well. Working with someone familiar with Cook County probate — even if they're not physically located in Rolling Meadows — is often more important than proximity.

## When Do You Need a Probate Attorney?

Not every estate requires formal probate, but most do when real property is involved. Here are the most common situations where Rolling Meadows families need legal help:

**The estate includes a home or real estate.** If your loved one owned property solely in their name, it typically must go through probate before it can be sold or transferred. Illinois law requires court authorization for the executor to deed the property.

**There is no will.** When someone dies intestate (without a will), Illinois law determines how assets are distributed. An attorney helps navigate the intestacy process and ensures the right heirs are identified.

**There are creditor claims or disputes.** If multiple creditors are making claims against the estate, or if family members disagree about the will or distribution, legal representation is essential.

**The estate has significant assets.** Larger estates — especially those with investment accounts, business interests, or multiple properties — benefit from professional administration to avoid costly mistakes.

## The Cook County Probate Timeline

For a straightforward Rolling Meadows estate, here's what to expect:

**Weeks 1–6:** File a petition at the Daley Center. The court appoints the executor and issues Letters of Office — the document that authorizes the executor to manage estate assets.

**Months 1–6:** The executor publishes a notice to creditors. Illinois gives creditors 6 months from publication to file claims against the estate.

**Months 6–14:** Once the creditor period closes, the executor pays valid debts, prepares a final accounting, and petitions the court for distribution to heirs.

Cook County's volume means filings must be precise. An attorney who handles Cook County probate regularly can keep your case on track and avoid delays.

## Selling a Home in Rolling Meadows Through Probate

Many Rolling Meadows probate estates involve selling a family home. The executor has the authority to list and sell the property on behalf of the estate — but only after receiving Letters of Office from the court.

Common situations we see:

- The property has been vacant for months while the estate is open
- Family members need help coordinating cleanout and preparation before listing
- The executor needs guidance on pricing and whether to sell traditionally or off-market

If any of this applies to your situation, getting the right team around you early — both a probate attorney and a real estate professional with probate experience — makes the process significantly smoother.
